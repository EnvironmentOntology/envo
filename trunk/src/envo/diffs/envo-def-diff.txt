CHEBI:15611 : sarcosine   
OLD: A N-methylglycine that has formula C3H7NO2.
NEW: The N-methyl derivative of glycine.


CHEBI:16199 : urea   
OLD: A monocarboxylic acid amide that has formula CH4N2O.
NEW: A carbonyl group with two C-bound amine groups.


CHEBI:16236 : ethanol   
OLD: An ethanol that has formula C2H6O.
NEW: A primary alcohol that is ethane in which one of the hydrogens is substituted by a hydroxy group.


CHEBI:16454 : pantothenate   
OLD: A pantothenate that has formula C9H16NO5.
NEW: A monocarboxylic acid anion that is the conjugate base of pantothenic acid, obtained by deprotonation of the carboxy group.


CHEBI:16482 : naphthalene   
OLD: An ortho-fused bicyclic arene that has formula C10H8.
NEW: An aromatic hydrocarbon comprising two fused benzene rings.


CHEBI:17347 : testosterone   
OLD: An androgen that has formula C19H28O2.
NEW: An androstanoid having 17beta-hydroxy and 3-oxo groups, together with unsaturation at C-4-C-5.


CHEBI:18139 : trimethylamine   
OLD: A methylamine that has formula C3H9N.
NEW: A tertiary amine that is ammonia in which each hydrogen atom is substituted by an methyl group.

CHEBI:33285 : heteroorganic entity   
OLD: A heteroorganic entity is one in which carbon atoms or organic groups are bonded directly to one or more heteroatoms.
NEW: A heteroorganic entity is an organic molecular entity in which carbon atoms or organic groups are bonded directly to one or more heteroatoms.


CHEBI:33575 : carboxylic acid   
OLD: An oxoacid having the structure RC(=O)OH. The term is used as a suffix in systematic name formation to denote the -C(=O)OH group including its carbon atom.
NEW: A carbon oxoacid acid carrying at least one -C(=O)OH group and having the structure RC(=O)OH, where R is any any monovalent functional group. Carboxylic acids are the most common type of organic acid.


CHEBI:35352 : organonitrogen compound   
OLD: A compound containing at least one carbon-nitrogen bond.
NEW: Any heteroorganic entity containing at least one carbon-nitrogen bond.


CHEBI:35715 : nitro compound   
OLD: A compound having the nitro group, -NO2 (free valence on nitrogen), which may be attached to carbon, nitrogen (as in nitramines), or oxygen (as in nitrates), among other elements (in the absence of specification, C-nitro compounds are usually implied).
NEW: A compound having a nitro group, -NO2 (free valence on nitrogen), which may be attached to carbon, nitrogen (as in nitramines), or oxygen (as in nitrates), among other elements (in the absence of specification, C-nitro compounds are usually implied).


CHEBI:35716 : C-nitro compound   
OLD: A compound having the nitro group (-NO2) attached to carbon atom.
NEW: A nitro compound having the nitro group (-NO2) attached to a carbon atom.


CHEBI:36963 : organooxygen compound   
OLD: An organooxygen compound is a compound containing at least one carbon-oxygen bond.
NEW: An organochalcogen compound containing at least one carbon-oxygen bond.


CHEBI:37622 : carboxamide   
OLD: Amides of carboxylic acids, having the structure RC(=O)NR2. The term is used as a suffix in systematic name formation to denote the -C(=O)NH2 group including its carbon atom.
NEW: An amide of a carboxylic acid, having the structure RC(=O)NR2. The term is used as a suffix in systematic name formation to denote the -C(=O)NH2 group including its carbon atom.
